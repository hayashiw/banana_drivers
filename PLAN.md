### Force objective findings (2026-04-21)

Source review of `simsopt/src/simsopt/field/force.py` and `selffield.py`. Cited claims are file:line into [hybrid_torus/banana/simsopt/](../simsopt/).

- **`LpCurveForce`** ([force.py:1215-1324](../simsopt/src/simsopt/field/force.py#L1215-L1324)) evaluates $J = (1/p)\sum_i (1/L_i)\oint \max(|d\vec F_i/d\ell_i| - F_0, 0)^p\,d\ell_i$ with $p$ and threshold $F_0$ as ctor arguments (default $p=2$, $F_0=0$). It includes the **regularized self-field** via the Landreman-Hurwitz scheme ([selffield.py:117-147](../simsopt/src/simsopt/field/selffield.py#L117-L147)) тАФ the on-curve singularity is subtracted analytically using a soft-core regularization distance set by the coil cross-section ($\delta = a^2/\sqrt{e}$ for circular, anisotropic for rectangular, [selffield.py:63-95](../simsopt/src/simsopt/field/selffield.py#L63-L95)). Constructor requires `RegularizedCoil` targets ([force.py:1268-1269](../simsopt/src/simsopt/field/force.py#L1268-L1269)).
- **`SquaredMeanForce`** ([force.py:897-906](../simsopt/src/simsopt/field/force.py#L897-L906)) evaluates $J = \sum_i (\oint \vec F_i\,d\ell_i / L_i)^2$ тАФ a scalar-averaged mutual-force penalty. It **excludes** self-field entirely ([force.py:874-883](../simsopt/src/simsopt/field/force.py#L874-L883); `_mutual_B_field_at_point_pure` is called with `exclude_index=i` on targets, [force.py:118-159](../simsopt/src/simsopt/field/force.py#L118-L159)). Works with plain `Coil` (no regularization metadata needed).
- **Discretization.** Both require uniform quadpoints within each group (targets / coarse sources / fine sources) and support independent `downsample`. Coarse/fine source split lets TF quadpoints differ from banana quadpoints without a resampling step. No requirement that banana quadpoints match TF quadpoints.
- **Gradients.** Both use `jax.grad` through `@derivative_dec` ([force.py:984-992](../simsopt/src/simsopt/field/force.py#L984-L992), [force.py:1305-1314](../simsopt/src/simsopt/field/force.py#L1305-L1314)). `LpCurveForce` additionally needs $\gamma''$ because the self-field kernel uses it.
- **Natural scale.** Test case at $I=100$ kA, $R_0=1.7$ m gives self-force per unit length $\sim 1$ MN/m ([test_selffieldforces.py:134-190](../simsopt/tests/field/test_selffieldforces.py#L134-L190)), i.e. $J_{\text{SMF}} \sim 1$ (MN/m)┬▓. For the banana case (80 kA TF, banana $I \in [5, 16]$ kA) the dominant force is TF-on-banana; a mutual-only estimate gives $J \sim 10^{-2}$ to $10^{-1}$ (MN/m)┬▓, so both objectives land 1тАУ2 orders above `NonQuasiSymmetricRatio` ($\sim 10^{-2}$) and need weight $\sim 10^{-2}$ тАУ $10^{-3}$.

**Recommendation (post-optimization evaluator only тАФ not added to the objective).** Report `LpCurveForce(p=2, threshold=0)` with banana coils as `target_coils` and TF coils as `source_coils_coarse`. Self-field inclusion matters for banana hoop stress (banana currents up to 16 kA with tight bending radius have non-negligible self-force), and `SquaredMeanForce`'s mutual-only formulation silently drops that term тАФ so the `SquaredMeanForce` value is worth logging alongside as a mutual-only reference, not as a substitute. `LpCurveForce` requires `RegularizedCoil` wrappers around the banana coils (cross-section $a$ from `hbt_parameters.py`); wrapper construction lives in `post_process.py` and does **not** touch the optimization graph. Units are (MN/m)┬▓ for `SquaredMeanForce` and (MN/m)$^p$ for `LpCurveForce`; post-processing should also report the peak $|d\vec F/d\ell|$ along each banana so the hardware team can read a max force per unit length directly.

**`dof_scale` is a coordinate rescaling, NOT a step bound (2026-04-09).** Investigation of SIMSOPT's `augmented_lagrangian.py` confirmed: `dof_scale` maps DOFs to a rescaled space ($y = x / \text{scale}$). Run 51253798 showed $\|\Delta x\|=0.102$ with `dof_scale=0.005` тАФ the raw L-BFGS-B direction had magnitude ~20, and 50 inner iterations accumulated 0.102 physical displacement. The only hard cap on total displacement per outer iteration is `maxfun`.

**BoozerLS `constraint_weight` bump 1.0 тЖТ 1.0e+3 (2026-04-09).** `boozer.constraint_weight` controls the weight on BoozerLS's surface-label penalty term relative to the Boozer residual in the least-squares problem. The larger the weight, the more firmly BoozerLS anchors the iota iterate to the requested label (via $(\iota - \iota_\text{label})^2$-type terms in the residual) rather than letting it drift to whatever minimizes the Boozer residual alone. The prior value `1.0` allowed drift into the spurious iota=0 basin on any surface with weak helical coupling to the coil field (see `diag_iota_basin.py` results + helical amplitude hypothesis above). `1.0e+3` matches SIMSOPT example conventions for stiffer anchoring. This is an **independent mitigation** from the coil capability problem тАФ it does NOT manufacture a basin that doesn't exist (Poincare 51287878 showed the coils can't support $\iota=0.15$ regardless of BoozerLS settings), but it should reduce the risk of BoozerLS wandering into degenerate iota=0 basins when the coil field DOES support the target, making future cold-start pipeline runs more reliable once a coil-capable $\iota_\text{max}$ is chosen. Applies to singlestage (`03_singlestage_driver.py`) and the standalone `utils/init_boozersurface.py` CLI which both read the same config key.

**Poincare `R_axis` starting-point patch (2026-04-09).** `poincare_tracing.py:make_start_points` previously estimated the magnetic axis $R$ as the arithmetic mean of inboard/outboard midplane points ($z\approx 0$) on the $\phi=0$ cross section, which assumes $R_\text{axis}$ is halfway between inboard and outboard тАФ only true for an up-down-symmetric ellipse with zero triangularity. For positive triangularity the true axis sits inboard of the midplane mean (Shafranov-like shift from shape alone). Switched to a weighted heuristic `r_axis = 0.25 * (3*r_inboard + r_outboard)` тАФ shift the starting-sweep origin 3:1 inboard тАФ so the starting R sweep covers the expected axis region instead of landing on the outboard side of it. Midplane mean is kept in the log for comparison. The quadpoint-centroid alternative (`cs0_r.mean()`) is present in the source as a commented reference for future experiments; the 3:1 heuristic was chosen because it gives a more conservative inward bias that handles circular-core surfaces (where the centroid equals the midplane mean) without regressing. Rationale: on surfaces whose $\iota$ is too small to support nesting much beyond the axis, getting the starting R close to the real axis matters.

Root cause: **SquaredFlux was the only non-self-clipping term in the constraint list**. Earlier (2026-04-08) this was done via `QuadraticPenalty(Jsqf, sqf_target, "max")` wrapping, but that created a ghost $\lambda/\mu$ ratchet (see 51251125, 51251674). **Stage 1 phiedge cache bug (2026-04-08).** Warm-start stage 1 was producing wouts with $|B|\sim 12\text{ T}$ (seed wout has ${\sim}1\text{ T}$). Root cause: `Vmec(mpi=mpi)` loads `input.default` at construction, which sets `phiedge=1.0`. The Optimizable `__init__` calls `self.get_dofs()` once and caches `[1.0, 0.0, 1.0]` in `local_full_x`. The driver then overrode `vmec.indata.phiedge = 0.083` from the seed wout тАФ but that write bypasses the DOF cache. When `least_squares_mpi_solve` later broadcasts the free-DOF vector via `prob.x = x`, the stale `[1.0, 0.0, 1.0]` silently gets written back through `Vmec.set_dofs`, restoring `phiedge=1.0`. **Stage 2 current collapse to 0 kA (job 51246996, 2026-04-08).** First stage 2 ALM run after adding `current` as a 5th constraint (`QuadraticPenalty(|I|, 20\text{ kA}, "max")`) as a warm-start-friendly soft cap. Root cause: the warm-start surface from stage 1 gives *lower* normalized SquaredFlux with TF alone ($J_\text{sqf}=1.85\text{e-}3$) than with TF + banana at the initial coil shape ($J_\text{sqf}=2.71\text{e-}3$), because the Fourier-initialized banana coil shape does not yet match what the plasma wants тАФ any nonzero current *adds* symmetry-breaking error on top of the near-symmetric TF field. The ALM descent correctly follows the gradient to $I=0$. Once there, the banana B-field is $I \cdot B_\text{unit}(\text{shape})$, so every shape gradient carries a factor of $I$ and vanishes. $\partial J/\partial I$ at $I=0$ is not exactly zero analytically, but `numpy.sign(0)=0` in `CurrentPenaltyWrapper.dJ()` plus the already-zero shape gradients pin the system at a degenerate stationary point. The trivial solution ($I=0$, all constraints 0) is lower-$J$ than anything the optimizer can reach by perturbing shapes.

## Augmented Lagrangian Method (ALM) тАФ Stage 2 port complete

**Motivation.** Stage 2 job 51219280 (2026-04-08) failed because L-BFGS-B's initial descent direction from a fresh `boozersurface.init.json` walked into the `LpCurvCurv` penalty cliff. The `p=4` curvature penalty with threshold 20 mтБ╗┬╣ creates a sharp nonlinear wall; once the optimizer is on the wall at iter 74, the recommended step blew J up by 14 orders of magnitude (9.6e-4 тЖТ 5.3e+10), line search could not recover, and the run terminated at machine precision with large gradient. This is starting-point-sensitive and not robust enough for Pareto scans.

**Dead end тАФ two-phase warmup (Option B).** Before ALM, a temporary two-phase driver was tried: Phase 1 minimized `SquaredFlux` alone (no penalty walls), Phase 2 enabled the full weighted objective. Phase 1 let the coils grow 11├Ч unconstrained (length 0.89 тЖТ 9.87 m, CC collapse to 4.2 mm, curvature 2197 mтБ╗┬╣); Phase 2 could not recover. `SquaredFlux` alone is pathologically unbounded тАФ this approach is dead. See job 51221431.

**ALM approach.** Set `f=None` and place **all** objectives тАФ including SquaredFlux тАФ in the constraint list. ALM treats each as an equality-style residual, ramps per-constraint penalty weights $\mu_i$ in an outer loop, and updates Lagrange multipliers $\lambda_i$ as constraints become satisfied. The effective weight on each constraint's gradient is $(\mu_i c_i - \lambda_i)$, analogous to $w_i$ in a traditional weighted objective. Inner loop is L-BFGS-B on a smooth augmented Lagrangian тАФ no penalty cliffs.
